# Memorandum — Apparent internal cross-reference errors in the N.D.C.C. and N.D.A.C.

**To:** Code Revisor, North Dakota Legislative Council (Legal Division)
**From:** [sender — see note below]
**Date:** June 13, 2026
**Re:** Cross-references in the Century Code and Administrative Code that point to sections which do not appear to exist — flagged for your review

---

## Purpose and scope

While building and validating a full-text database of North Dakota primary law, I ran an automated *cross-reference resolver* over the Century Code (N.D.C.C.) and Administrative Code (N.D.A.C.): it parses every internal "section/chapter NN-NN-NN" reference in the text of each section and checks whether the cited target actually exists in the code. Of roughly 40,000 references, more than 99% resolve cleanly. This memo lists the small set that do **not** — references that, as published, point to a section number that does not exist in the cited chapter.

I am flagging these for the Council's review rather than asserting they are errors. Each could be (a) a typographical slip in the published text, (b) a deliberate reference to a section that was repealed, renumbered, or never enacted, or (c) — in the hazardous-waste rules — an artifact of the way the federal RCRA standards are incorporated by reference. Only the revisor's office can say which. Where the surrounding text makes the drafter's intent reasonably clear, I have offered a likely intended target and a confidence label; where it does not, I have said so.

**Verification method.** Every item below was confirmed by hand against the official chapter PDFs on ndlegis.gov (Century Code: `ndlegis.gov/cencode/`; Administrative Code: `ndlegis.gov/information/acdata/pdf/`). The quoted language is the published text; the "does not exist" conclusions are based on the section headings actually present in the cited chapter as of June 13, 2026. I used a second, independent PDF text extractor (poppler) so the findings do not depend on my database's own ingestion.

**Confidence labels:** *High* = intended target is clear from context and exists. *Moderate* = the reference is almost certainly wrong and a likely target is identifiable but not certain. *Low* = the reference appears wrong but I cannot responsibly guess the intended target.

### Notes on Part I

- **N.D.C.C. § 28-22-03.1 (item 1):** The reference sits in a list of qualified-domestic-relations-order provisions for the three public retirement systems (N.D.C.C. § 15-39.1-12.2, N.D.C.C. § 54-52-17.6). The matching provision for the Highway Patrolmen's retirement system is **N.D.C.C. § 39-03.1-14.2** ("Benefit payments to alternate payee under qualified domestic relations order"), which exists; N.D.C.C. § 39-03-14.2 (a motor-vehicle title) does not. Notably, **two other subsections of N.D.C.C. § 28-22-03.1 itself cite the same provision correctly as "39-03.1-14.2,"** so this appears to be an isolated dropped ".1" in one of the three parallel citations.
- **N.D.C.C. § 41-09-73 (item 2):** N.D.C.C. § 11-18-23 is captioned "Filing or recording documents with recorder — Social security numbers," which is exactly the subject of the citing sentence (social-security numbers in the real-property records).
- **N.D.C.C. § 23.1-06-07 (item 4):** N.D.C.C. § 23.1-01-04 ("Rulemaking authority — Limitations") is cited three other times in this same section without the ".1"; the lone "23.1-01-04.1" appears to be a stray suffix.

### Note on the hazardous-waste rules (chapter 33.1-24)

Items 7–11 all sit in chapter 33.1-24, which incorporates the federal RCRA standards (40 C.F.R.). A recurring pattern there is a reference to a chapter **-04** or **-06** section that does not exist, where the **same number in chapter -05 does** (chapter -05 has 1,169 sections; -04 has 8; -06 has 100). Items 7–9 (the "-04-" references) are well-supported as chapter-digit slips to "-05-," especially item 7, where the slip breaks an otherwise-coherent section range. Item 11 (the "-06-235" reference) is different: the sentence cites both the -05 and the -06 version as distinct provisions, consistent with the parallel 40 C.F.R. Part 264 / Part 265 structure — so it may be an intended reference to a Part-265-parallel section that was not separately codified, rather than a typo. I defer entirely to your office on the incorporation scheme here.
