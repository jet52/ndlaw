

12 N.D. 504
Supreme Court of North Dakota.
MONTGOMERY
v.
TUCKER.
Aug. 7, 1903.
Synopsis
Appeal from District Court, Richland County; Lauder, Judge. Action by George S. Montgomery, as receiver, against George A. Tucker. From a judgment for plaintiff, defendant appeals. Reversed.
 
Attorneys and Law Firms
*1134 Lee Combs, for appellant.
Purcell & Bradley and Charles E. Wolfe, for respondent.
Opinion

PER CURIAM.

This case is not distinguishable in any way upon its facts from the case of Montgomery v. Whitbeck (just decided by this court) 96 N. W. 327. The case was tried in the district court on the same day, by the same counsel, and before the same judge as was the Whitbeck Case. The purpose of the action is the same. The pleadings and proofs are alike in essential particulars, and the case is in all respects controlled by the decision of that controversy. The judgment of the district court is reversed. That court is directed to reverse its judgment and to dismiss the action. Appellant will recover costs of all courts.
