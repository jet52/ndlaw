

49 N.D. 933
Supreme Court of North Dakota.
OLNESS
v.
DUFFY et al.
June 11, 1923.

Syllabus by the Court.
An order for judgment is not appealable under section 7841, Comp. Laws 1913. Appeal dismissed on the authority of Malherek v. City of Fargo, 190 N. W. 176, and other cases therein cited.
 
Synopsis
Appeal from District Court, McLean County; Fred Jansonius, Judge.
 
Action by Ole Olness against James J. Duffy and another. From a portion of an order entered, plaintiff appeals. Appeal dismissed.
 
Attorneys and Law Firms
*113 B. A. Dickinson, of Ryder, for appellant.
E. T. Burke, of Bismarck, for respondents.
Opinion

PER CURIAM.

The notice of appeal in the instant case is to the effect that the plaintiff appeals from a part of an order made which orders that the defendants are entitled to the possession of the wheat described in the proceedings and which concludes with the statement, “Let judgment be entered accordingly.” The appeal is clearly from a part of the order for judgment. Under the statute and under numerous decisions of this court, such an order is not appealable. Comp. Laws 1913, § 7841; Malherek v. City of Fargo (N. D.) 190 N. W. 176; Dibble v. Hanson, 17 N. D. 21, 114 N. W. 371, 16 Ann. Cas. 1210; Lough v. White, 13 N. D. 387, 100 N. W. 1084; Weber Case, 4 N. D. 119, 59 N. W. 523, 28 L. R. A. 621; Field v. Elevator Co., 5 N. D. 400, 67 N. W. 147.
 
There being nothing before this court for review, it follows that the appeal must be dismissed. It is so ordered.
 
W. J. KNEESHAW, District Judge, sitting in place of NUESSLE, J., not participating.
