## 9 · Definition of "fully validated"

An opinion is considered validated when:
- It has at least two independent source texts linked in `opinion_sources` (OR it sits in the 1953–1996 single-source era and has been either auto-verified against Westlaw Quick Check or manually spot-checked), AND
- Its author, date_filed, and case_name have been confirmed against a non-OCR source (ndcourts.gov, Westlaw, or bound reporter), AND
- `overall_score >= 0.5`, AND
- Parallel citations are populated from metadata, not just the ingest source.

**PDF era (1997+ ndcourts.gov) — ratified policy 2026-05-18.** The court PDF is the authoritative copy; the linked markdown is the analyzer-extracted text of that same PDF (one chain, NOT an independent second source — see SCHEMA/memory provenance). Policy:
- **Use a second source whenever available**, including proactively downloading a Westlaw copy to obtain one.
- **A Westlaw copy is MANDATORY for any PDF-era opinion whose text required OCR to extract** (scanned/imaged PDF, no embedded text layer). For cleanly-extracted embedded-text PDFs a single court-PDF-derived source is acceptable as authoritative.
- "Required OCR" signal (resolved 2026-05-18): authoritative detector is **court-PDF structure** — a scanned/OCR-required PDF carries ~one full-page raster image per page; born-digital carries ~none (`triage/classify_pdf_extraction.py`). ## 10 · Planned enhancement — synthetic medium-neutral cite as universal unique ID

**Goal.** Extend the post-1997 medium-neutral citation format (`YYYY ND N` — e.g. `1997 ND 201`, `2020 ND 30`) **backward to every opinion**, including pre-1997, and use it as the corpus's stable unique identifier.

**Rationale.** Pre-1997 opinions were not published with the `YYYY ND N` neutral format, and the reporter-page cite is *not* a unique key in that era — the old per-curiam clusters print multiple distinct short opinions on one shared N.D./N.W. page (e.g. *Sykes v. Allen* and *Montgomery v. Tucker* both at `12 N.D. 504, 96 N.W. 1134`; see the #1–#5 flagged-collision work). A back-assigned `YYYY ND N` gives each opinion — including each member of a shared-page cluster — a single unambiguous identifier, and lets `neutral_cite_uniqueness` extend corpus-wide instead of only 1997+.

**Design sketch (not final):**
- Assign `YYYY ND N` where `YYYY` = filing year and `N` = sequence within that year, ordered by `date_filed`, then N.D. page, then **on-page order for shared-page clusters**.
- **Shared-page disambiguation:** each distinct opinion on a multi-opinion bound page gets its own sequence number — that is the mechanism that finally makes shared-page opinions individually addressable (resolves the lookup-ambiguity limitation noted in the shared-page model discussion).
- **Provenance is mandatory.** Back-assigned cites are *synthetic/editorial*, not how the case was published. They MUST be stored and surfaced as distinctly synthetic (separate reporter type / flag), never presented as an original citation. Misrepresenting a 1903 opinion as natively `1903 ND 47` would violate the accuracy/fair-notice bar that drives the authoritative-text goal — a court adopting the corpus must be able to tell native neutral cites (1997+) from back-assigned ones.
- **Human review required:** bound-volume pages carrying multiple opinions must be human-reviewed to fix the correct on-page publication order before sequence numbers are assigned (order must reflect true publication order, not ingest order).

**Open design questions:** storage (dedicated `neutral_cite` column on `opinions` vs a flagged `citations` row of a new reporter type); interaction with the existing 258-pair `neutral_cite_uniqueness` baseline once scope extends pre-1997; renumbering policy if an opinion is later inserted/removed (stability vs density); how synthetic cites appear in `lookup_opinion`/`cited_by` resolution. To be specified before implementation; capture the settled contract in the future `SCHEMA.md`.

**Design RATIFIED 2026-05-19.** Settled in CHANGELOG-data.md `nw-bound-image-source-section10-spotcheck-2026-05-19`:
- Storage: `citations` row with new taxonomy value `ND-neutral-synthetic` (extends Contract 1 enum); `is_primary=0` always.
- Format: `YYYY ND nnn`; ordering key `(date_filed, ND-starting-page, NW-starting-page, on-page-order)`.
- Visual marker: bracketed `[YYYY ND nnn]` in MCP / `lookup_opinion` output to distinguish from native cites.
- Scope: all pre-1997 (`date_filed < '1997-01-01'`, ~14,000 opinions); the 258 post-1997 neutral_cite_uniqueness baseline is §6 not §10.

## 14 · Citation-graph integrity — forward-cite defects (added 2026-05-29)

**Source:** random audit of 50 opinions decided in the last 3 years (2023-05-29..2026-05-29), full text read against the stored `text_citations` ("cases cited") and `cited_by` ("cited by") fields. Reproducible sample + per-case findings + headline prevalence (re-verified in SQL, corpus-wide) in `triage/cite-audit-2026-05-29/` (`REPORT.md`, `sample.json`, `select_sample.py`, `dump_case.py`).

**Top-line:** `cited_by` (the reverse graph) is **clean** — 0 bad edges in the sample, 0 self-edges corpus-wide. Every defect is in the **forward** extraction/display layer. None of these are dangerous to fix (no opinion text is touched), but they materially degrade `get_cited_authorities` for essentially every modern case, so they belong above the long-tail cleanup.

**Verified prevalence (corpus-wide unless noted):**
| Defect | Sample | Corpus |
|---|---|---|
| Self-citation (case lists its own cite as an authority) | 49/50 cases | 31,331 rows / 19,759 opinions |
| Jurisdiction mistag: N.W.x cite to an ND case tagged ≠ `nd` | 433 | 110,164 |
| `antecedent_name` NULL on case-cites | 37/50 all-NULL; 0 fully populated | 95% of case-cites; only 1,767/19,788 opinions have any |
| Reporter-truncation phantoms (`419 F.2d`→`419 F. 2`, `So.`/`F.4th` too) | 8 | 1,022 |
| Parallel-cite duplication (neutral + N.W. as two rows) | pervasive | `parallel_group` filled on only 127,752/339,822 |

**Durable solutions, in leverage order (each logged to `changelog` + CHANGELOG-data.md per policy):**

- [x] **(a) Tool-layer dedup in `get_cited_authorities`** (`server.py`). **DONE 2026-05-29.** `nd_cases` is now deduped by resolved `oid`: a case cited via both its neutral and N.W. cite is ONE entry — canonical `cite` (neutral preferred) plus the rest in a new additive `parallel_cites` field; the subject opinion's own `oid` is excluded (defense-in-depth). Verified: 2023 ND 103 `in_corpus_cases` 34→23, 0 duplicate oids, no self-listing; admin order 2025 ND 144 →0. Pure display-layer change — no data migration. Covers the user-facing half of the self-cite (#b) and the resolved-case half of the parallel-dupe (#e) problems corpus-wide. **Note: the running MCP server process must be reloaded to serve the new behavior.**
- [x] **(b) Self-citation guard + cleanup** (`cite_extract`). **DONE 2026-05-29** (batch `strip-self-citations-2026-05-29`). Deleted **30,712 unambiguous** self-cites (cite unique to the opinion); **left 619 shared-page** cites for the shared-page pass. Added guard in `_store_results` (skip insert when the cite is the opinion's own + unique to it; verified it blocks re-introduction) and invariant `no_self_citation` (baseline 0). `text_citations` 339,822→309,110; invariants 23/2/0; snapshot `opinions.db.bak-pre-strip-self-cites-2026-05-29.zst`. Detail in CHANGELOG-data.md.
- [x] **(c) reporter-truncation cleanup** (batch `strip-truncation-phantoms-2026-05-29`). **DONE 2026-05-29.** *Finding:* the jetcite parser is **already fixed in 2.1.0** — verified `419 F.2d 1197`/`892 So. 2d 1075`/`43 F.4th 100` parse correctly and genuine 1st-series `200 F. 100` is still caught, so **no jetcite change was needed**; the phantoms were stale rows from an older version that `INSERT OR IGNORE` never cleared. Deleted **1,650 sibling-confirmed** phantoms (F. 883, S.W. 195, So. 124, S.E. 116, Cal. 20, …) — only where the opinion also held the correct full cite; **held 191** no-sibling rows (real `S. Ct.`/1st-series cites + a separate garbage-volume defect `006 N.D. 3`). `text_citations` 309,110→307,460; invariants 23/2/0; snapshot taken. Detail in CHANGELOG-data.md. **Spun off:** garbage leading-zero volumes (`00 U.S. 3`, `006 N.D. 3`) are a distinct parsing defect — see (h) below.
- [ ] **(h) Garbage-volume cites** (new, found during (c)). A handful of `text_citations` carry malformed volumes with leading zeros (`00 U.S. 3`, `000 U.S. 3`, `006 N.D. 3`) — almost certainly OCR/parse artifacts. Scope and either correct (strip leading zeros if a real cite is recoverable) or delete (if garbage). Small; low priority.
- [ ] **(d) `antecedent_name` coverage + correctness.** *Why:* `antecedent_name` is the party name that powers cited_by shared-page disambiguation ([[reference_jetcite_coupling]]) and the "cases cited" display, but it is NULL for 95% of case-cites — only ~9% of opinions were scanned with the antecedent-capable jetcite (≥2.1.0). Where populated, the span is often wrong: corporate suffix only (`Inc.` 483, `LLC` 118, `L.P.`, `P.C.`, `Co.`, `Corp.`), a stray leading `In ` (~214, vs. legitimate `In re` 1,140), a tail jurisdiction word (`Divide Cnty.`), or a cross-sentence fragment (`Walsh County Recorder. See State v. Cleavenger`). **Fix:** (i) jetcite — consume the full party-name run (don't terminate on a corporate suffix), strip leading connectors (`In `/`See `/`Cf. `/`e.g., ` but KEEP `In re`/`In the Matter of`/`Interest of`), never cross a sentence boundary; (ii) re-run `cite_extract` corpus-wide once jetcite is fixed to backfill coverage.
- [~] **(e) Parallel-cite collapse.** *Why:* modern ND cases are cited as "2024 ND 110, ¶ X, 7 N.W.3d 253" and stored as two rows; the `parallel_group` column exists for this but is filled on only 38% of rows and is ignored by the tool. Recording each cite string is fine; showing/counting the case twice is not. **PARTIALLY DONE 2026-05-29:** (a)'s `oid` dedup collapses all *resolved in-corpus* parallels (the ND-case majority). **Remaining:** `other_cases` (out-of-corpus parallels — e.g. U.S. + S.Ct. + L.Ed. of one SCOTUS case) are still listed separately; populate `parallel_group` during extraction and collapse those groups in the tool.
- [x] **(f) Jurisdiction backfill.** **DONE 2026-05-29** (batch `jurisdiction-backfill-2026-05-29`). Set `jurisdiction='nd'` for **106,961** in-corpus N.W. case cites (N.W.2d 98,656 / N.W. 8,301 / N.W.3d 4) — any N.W. cite resolving to a corpus opinion is definitionally ND (unique volume+page). Unresolved N.W. cites keep `us`. Made durable in `cite_extract` (resolved case cites stored `nd` at insert; verified by re-scan). Snapshot-reversible (see CHANGELOG-data.md for the per-row-logging rationale); invariants 23/2/0.
- [ ] **(g) Recognizer omissions** (jetcite; partially overlaps §13). *Why:* the few genuine omissions clustered in forms jetcite doesn't recognize — **spelled-out constitutional refs** ("the Sixth Amendment of the United States Constitution", "Article I, § 12 of the North Dakota Constitution"; note this hits *modern* opinions too, complementing §13's pre-1981 focus), **statute lists/ranges** ("§§ 28-01-07 and 28-01-08" captures only the first; also §§ 9-03-04/-24, §§ 29-32.1-09.1/-12), and **prose rule references** ("Rule 11 of the North Dakota Rules of Criminal Procedure", "Rules of Appellate Procedure 28 and 30"). Foreign authorities (CA/Idaho codes) and a cited case's N.W. parallel (`778 N.W.2d 786`) were also occasionally missed — lower priority. **Fix:** extend jetcite recognizers accordingly.
